Case studies

Real governance failures from a production decision system — each one caught, costed, and closed with a mechanical rule. The misses are the product: they show the accountability loop actually runs.

Governance failure caught in production · Apr 2026 · stop-discipline rule origin

When the stop moves down instead of selling

Price broke three consecutive stop levels; the system silently rewrote the stop each time and kept adding to the loser. The fix made silent stop-lowering a build error.
State-drift failure · Apr 2026 · positions-as-single-source-of-truth rule origin

The system recommended selling a position that no longer existed

A SELL recommendation shipped for a ticker fully exited three weeks earlier — and sat unchallenged for nine days. The fix made every action row reconcile against the canonical holdings file.
Behavioral failure · Mar–Apr 2026 · stop-only-on-real-risk rule origin

Selling the winner, then buying it back higher

A reflexive 'lock profits' trim sold a compounding winner at $420 — which was then re-bought at $435 to $480 as the thesis kept working. The fix: no stop or trim without a dated bearish signal.
Data-integrity failure · Apr 2026 · avoid-list-source-of-truth rule origin

64 warnings, 30 of them about stocks nobody was looking at

The 'do not buy' list had 64 rows — 30 of them for tickers not even in that day's screened universe, with stale numbers copied by hand. The fix: derived membership, derived numbers, zero hand-typed claims.

Every incident above left receipts in the verified ledger; the fixes are enforced by build-time QC, not by promises.
