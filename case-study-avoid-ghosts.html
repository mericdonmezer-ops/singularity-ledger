Data-integrity failure · Apr 2026 · avoid-list-source-of-truth rule origin

64 warnings, 30 of them about stocks nobody was looking at

The daily output includes an avoid list — names the system evaluated and rejected, with reasons. An Apr 29 2026 audit found it had quietly rotted: of 64 rows, 30 named tickers that were not in that day's screened universe at all. They were inherited from previous days and never re-validated.

The deeper problem: hand-typed numbers

The same audit cross-checked every numeric claim inside the avoid reasons ("+47% in 5 days", "$15.61") against the live screener data:

Cross-check resultRows
Claim matched live data1
Claim contradicted live data (≥5pp off)15
Ticker not in the day's universe at all18

Accuracy of hand-authored ticker-level numbers: under 3%. Meanwhile the macro numbers on the same page — copied mechanically from a live data feed — were 7 for 7. The lesson was structural: narrative fields written by the reasoning layer drift; derived fields do not.

Governance fix (two rules): (1) avoid-list membership must trace to the current day's screened universe, the current holdings, or an explicit policy overlap — a pruning pass removes ghosts before render. (2) No numbers in narrative: ticker-level prices and returns are banned from free-text fields and rendered from the structured data source at build time. QC tags: AVOID-GHOST-TICKER, AVOID-EMBEDDED-NUMBER.

Why a governance buyer should care

Nothing here lost money directly — that is what makes it dangerous. Stale rejection lists and drifted numbers fail silently and erode the operator's trust calibration: if 47% of the warnings are noise, the operator learns to skim past all of them, including the real one. The ledger's job is to keep every published row traceable to a live source, so attention stays a scarce resource spent on true signals.

The before/after is in the chain: avoid-list row counts drop on the day the prune landed, and post-rule rows carry no embedded numerics.
