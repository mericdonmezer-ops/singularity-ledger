When the stop moves down instead of selling

Rheinmetall (RHM) was a defense anchor opened Mar 2026. Price drifted below three consecutive stop levels across four daily runs. The system lowered the stop each time instead of executing the documented trim — then kept issuing ADD signals on the same losing thesis.

Cost of the anti-pattern

  • Intended: −3% stop-out on Apr 20 breach → trim 3 shares, −3% realized.
  • Actual: open hole grew to −13% unrealized while the book chased the loser with ADDs.
  • User caught it Apr 24: "stop sessizce 3× aşağı çekildi" — classic moving-the-stop behavior.
Governance fix (stop-discipline rule): stops may tighten, never loosen without a dated, testable thesisRevision. If price breaches the published stop and no revision exists → mechanical exit row, not a rewritten stop. Build QC tag: STOP LOWERED SILENTLY.

What the ledger would show today

Each run's stop level and ADD recommendation are separate receipts. A verifier sees:

  1. ACTION rows recommending ADD while price is below the prior stop — contradiction on its face.
  2. No EXIT row when the stop was hit — accountability gap.
  3. After the rule: either TRIM at breach or an explicit revision log; silent rewrites fail QC.

This is why the product sells process integrity, not picks. The miss stays visible; the rule change is mechanized so the same failure mode cannot hide in narrative drift.
